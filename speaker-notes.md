## Slide 2: Two ways to win

**[0:30-1:30]**

> Poker has exactly two ways to win a hand. First: have the best hand when cards are compared. Second: bet in a way that makes everyone else give up. That second path is what separates poker from most card games.

---

## Slide 3: Your 7 cards

**[1:30-2:30]**

> Here's the setup. You get 2 private cards, called hole cards. Then 5 community cards are dealt face-up in the center, shared by all players. From those 7 total, you pick the best possible 5-card hand. You don't have to use both hole cards. That'll make more sense once we see examples.

---

## Slide 4: The deck

**[2:30-3:30]**

> Standard 52-card deck. Four suits, 13 ranks each. Suits have no ranking in Hold'em. If two players both have a flush, the one with higher cards wins, not a "better" suit. Ace is highest. It can also work as a 1 in the lowest possible straight, A-2-3-4-5. That's the only exception.

---

## Slide 5: Hand rankings (rare)

**[3:30-5:00]**

> Here are the top 5 hand rankings, best to worst. Royal flush, straight flush, four of a kind: incredibly rare. You might play a thousand hands and never see one. Full house and flush are strong but not common. Don't worry about memorizing all this. Focus on the next slide.

---

## Slide 6: Hand rankings (common)

**[5:00-6:30]**

> These are the hands that actually decide pots. One pair is the most common winning hand. Two pair is strong. Three of a kind is very strong. If you remember just these five, you're set.

---

## Slide 7: 7 choose 5

**[6:30-7:30]**

> Quick math for the quant-minded. 7 choose 5 is 21. So from your 7 cards, there are 21 different 5-card hands you could make. The best one counts. And across all possible deals, there are over 133 million unique 7-card combinations. That's the denominator for the probabilities on the next slide.

---

## Slide 8: Hand probabilities

**[7:30-9:00]**

> Here's the distribution. One pair shows up almost 44% of the time. Two pair another 23%. Together they make up two thirds of all hands. High card, meaning you made nothing, happens 17%. Everything from three of a kind up is under 5%. Royal flush is 1 in 30,000. Calibrate your expectations around these green bars, not the red ones.

---

## Slide 9: 5 of 7 example

**[9:00-10:00]**

> Concrete example. Your hand: K♥ 9♦. Board: K♠ 7♣ 3♦ 9♠ 2♥. Both Kings pair up, both Nines pair up. Your best 5 is Two Pair with a 7 kicker. The 3 and 2 are dimmed because they don't contribute.

## Slide 10: The table

**[10:00-10:30]**

> Standard poker table. 6 to 9 players around an oval. Community cards go face-up in the middle. You're highlighted at the bottom.

---

## Slide 11: Dealer button

**[10:30-11:00]**

> A white disc called the dealer button sits in front of one player each hand. Everything about who pays and who acts first is defined relative to this button.

---

## Slide 12: Blinds

**[11:00-12:00]**

> Before cards are dealt, the two seats left of the button must post forced bets called blinds. Small blind puts in half the minimum, big blind puts in the full amount. Without blinds, nobody would have incentive to play.

## Slide 14: Five actions

**[13:00-14:00]**

> Five choices. Fold: you're out. Check: pass without betting, only if nobody bet yet. Call: match what's there. Bet: put chips in first. Raise: increase someone else's bet. Every decision in poker is one of these five.

---

## Slide 15: Anatomy of a hand

**[14:00-15:00]**

> Five stages. Card is dealt or revealed, then everyone bets. Most hands don't make it to showdown. If everyone folds to one player, they win immediately. Let's walk through a complete hand.

## Slide 23: The 5-of-7 trap

**[25:30-27:30]**

> Board is A-A-K-K-Q. Player A has an Ace. Player B has two Kings.

**ASK:** "Who wins? Take your time."

Wait 15-20 seconds, then click.

> Player A has a full house. Aces full of Kings. Three Aces, two Kings. But Player B has FOUR Kings. Two from the board, two from their hand. Four of a kind beats a full house.

## Common questions

**"Can you use just one hole card?"**
Yes. You pick the best 5 from 7. You can use 2, 1, or 0 of your hole cards.

**"What if two people have the same hand?"**
Split pot. Suits don't break ties.

**"What's the minimum raise?"**
At least the size of the previous bet or raise. If BB is 2, min raise is to 4.

**"What does 'No Limit' mean?"**
You can bet any amount up to all your chips at any time.

**"What if you run out of chips mid-hand?"**
You go all-in and can only win a side pot up to what you put in. Don't go deep on this. Say "we'll cover that when it comes up."
