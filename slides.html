Overview

Two ways to win

Best hand

Make the strongest 5-card poker hand at showdown.

Last one standing

Everyone else folds. You win the pot without ever showing your cards.

Poker has exactly two ways to win. First: have the best hand when cards are compared. Second: bet in a way that makes everyone else give up. That second path is what separates poker from most card games.
Setup

Your 7 cards

2 hole cards — private

+

5 community cards — shared

7 total cards → pick your best 5

You don't have to use both hole cards. You can use 2, 1, or even 0.

Everyone gets 2 private cards, called hole cards. Then 5 cards are dealt face-up in the center, shared by all players. From those 7 total, you pick the best possible 5-card hand. You don't have to use both hole cards. That trips people up at first, but it'll make sense once we see examples.

Standard 52-card deck. Four suits, 13 ranks per suit. Important: suits have no ranking in Hold'em. If two players both have a flush, the one with higher-ranked cards wins, not the one with a "better" suit. Ace is highest. It can also act as a 1 in the lowest possible straight: A-2-3-4-5.

The Math

7 choose 5

From 7 cards, how many ways can you pick 5?

C(7,5)  =  7! / (5! × 2!)  =  21

There are 21 possible 5-card combinations from your 7 cards.
The dealer (or the software) automatically picks the best one for you.

From a full deck: C(52,7) = 133,784,560

That's how many unique 7-card deals are possible.

Quick math for the quant-minded. 7 choose 5 is 21. So from your 7 cards, there are 21 different 5-card hands you could make. The best one counts. And across all possible deals from a 52-card deck, there are over 133 million unique 7-card combinations. That's the sample space for the probability table we're about to see.
The Math

How likely is each hand?

Probability when dealt 7 random cards, best 5 of 7:

  • One Pair43.8%
  • Two Pair23.5%
  • High Card17.4%
  • Three of a Kind4.83%
  • Straight4.62%
  • Flush3.03%
  • Full House2.60%
  • Four of a Kind0.17%
  • Straight Flush0.028%
  • Royal Flush0.003%
This is the distribution. One pair shows up almost 44% of the time. Two pair another 23%. Together those two make up two-thirds of all hands. High card, meaning you made nothing, happens 17%. Everything from three of a kind up is under 5%. Four of a kind, straight flush, and royal flush combined are 0.2%. Calibrate your expectations around the big slices, not the small ones.

Before cards are dealt, the two seats immediately left of the button must post forced bets called blinds. The small blind puts in half the minimum, the big blind puts in the full minimum. In a 1/2 game, that means 1 chip and 2 chips. These go straight into the pot. Without blinds, nobody would have any incentive to play a hand.

Actions

Your five options

On every betting round, you pick one of these:

Fold
Give up your hand. You're out of this pot.
Check
Pass without betting. Only if nobody has bet yet.
Call
Match the current bet to stay in.
Bet
Put chips in first when nobody else has.
Raise
Increase someone else's bet.

Every decision you'll ever make in poker comes down to these five.

Five choices, every single round. Fold means you're done with this hand. Check means you pass without putting money in, but only if nobody has bet yet. Call means matching what's out there. Bet means you're the first one to put chips in. Raise means someone already bet and you're increasing it. That's the whole vocabulary.
Game Flow

Anatomy of a hand

Preflop / Flop / Turn / River / Showdown

Preflop
2 hole cards dealt. Betting round.

Flop
3 community cards revealed. Betting round.

Turn
4th community card. Betting round.

River
5th and final community card. Betting round.

Showdown
Remaining players reveal cards. Best hand wins.

A hand can end at any stage if everyone folds to one player.

Let's walk through a full hand with 4 players: Alice (Dealer), Bob (SB), Carol (BB), You.

Every hand follows this flow. Five stages. A card is dealt or revealed, then everyone bets. Repeat. But most hands don't reach showdown. If at any point everyone folds except one player, that player wins immediately. Let's walk through a complete hand step by step.
Preflop / Flop / Turn / River / Showdown

Bob posts SB (1), Carol posts BB (2). Everyone gets 2 cards.

Your cards:

A♥ K♦
Preflop betting order: starts left of the Big Blind (that's you).

You: Raise to 6

Alice: Fold

Bob: Call 6 (adds 5 to his blind of 1)

Carol: Call 6 (adds 4 to her blind of 2)

Pot: 18 chips
Blinds go in. Everyone gets 2 cards face down. You look at yours: Ace-King. Strong. Preflop, action starts left of the big blind. That's you. You raise to 6. Alice folds. Bob calls by adding 5 more on top of his 1 blind. Carol calls by adding 4. Pot is 18. Three players to the flop.

K♠ 7♣ 3♦

Bob: Check

Three cards hit the board: King-7-3. You have a King in hand and a King on the board. Pair of Kings. Now notice: postflop, action starts left of the DEALER, not the big blind. That means Bob goes first now. Bob checks, Carol checks. You bet 8. Bob folds, Carol calls. Pot is 34.

Practice

Who wins?

10♥ 10♣ 5♠ 8♦ 2♣
Player A: A♠ 10♦
Player B: K♥ 5♥

Player A: Three 10s (10♥ 10♣ 10♦ A♠ 8♦)
Player B: Two Pair, 10s and 5s (10♥ 10♣ 5♠ 5♥ K♥)

Player A wins. Three of a kind beats two pair.

Pop quiz. Board has two 10s, a 5, an 8, and a 2. Player A holds A-10. Player B holds K-5. Ask the room before clicking. Player A has three 10s. One from their hand, two from the board. Player B has two pair, 10s and 5s. Three of a kind beats two pair. Player A takes it.
Practice

The fold win

Preflop: Player A raises. Player B calls.

Flop: J♠ 8♥ 4♣  Player A bets. Player B calls.

Turn: Q♦  Player A bets big. Player B calls.

River: 6♣  Player A shoves all-in.

Player B: Fold

Player A wins. Cards are never shown.

Player A might have had the nuts. Player A might have had nothing.
When everyone folds, the winner never has to reveal.
This is the other way to win. Player A bets every street and goes all-in on the river. Player B decides it's not worth it and folds. Hand over. Player A takes the pot. And here's the thing: we never see what Player A had. Could have been a great hand. Could have been complete air. That ambiguity is what makes poker interesting.
Practice

The 5-of-7 trap

A♠ A♣ K♦ K♣ Q♥
Player A: A♥ 2♦
Player B: K♥ K♠

This one is hard. Take a minute.

Player A: Full House, Aces full of Kings (A♠ A♣ A♥ K♦ K♣)
Player B: Four Kings (K♦ K♣ K♥ K♠ A♠)

Player B wins. Four of a kind beats a full house.

Player B has all four Kings: two from the board, two from their hand. Always count all 7.

The hard one. Board has A-A-K-K-Q. Player A has an Ace. Player B has two Kings. Let the room think. Click to reveal: Player A has a full house, Aces full of Kings. Three Aces, two Kings. Sounds great. But Player B has four Kings. Two from the board plus two from their hand. Four of a kind beats a full house. This is why 5-of-7 matters. You have to consider all combinations.

Recap

What you now know

1. Best 5 of 7 cards wins. 2 hole cards + 5 community.

2. 10 hand rankings. Pair and two pair decide most pots.

3. Blinds create action. The button rotates so everyone pays.

4. Four betting rounds: preflop, flop, turn, river.

5. Five actions: fold, check, call, bet, raise.

6. You can win by making everyone else fold. No cards shown.

Fold Check Call Bet Raise
Six things. Best 5 of 7. Know the common hand rankings. Blinds rotate. Four betting rounds. Five possible actions. And you can win without the best hand if everyone folds. That's the complete ruleset for No Limit Hold'em.

Cheat Sheet

Hand Rankings (best to worst)
  • Royal Flush — A K Q J 10, same suit
  • Straight Flush — 5 in a row, same suit
  • Four of a Kind — 4 of same rank
  • Full House — 3 of a kind + pair
  • Flush — 5 same suit
  • Straight — 5 in a row
  • Three of a Kind — 3 of same rank
  • Two Pair — 2 different pairs
  • One Pair — 2 of same rank
  • High Card — nothing, best card plays
Betting Actions
  • Fold   Give up, out of the hand
  • Check   Pass (only if no bet yet)
  • Call   Match the current bet
  • Bet   First chips in
  • Raise   Increase someone's bet

Game Flow
  • Preflop — 2 hole cards, bet from left of BB
  • Flop — 3 board cards, bet from left of Dealer
  • Turn — 4th card, same as flop
  • River — 5th card, same as flop
  • Showdown — Best 5 of 7 wins
